## Response

**The Boy Who Practiced in the Forest**

Once there was a boy named Ekalavya who lived near a great forest. He loved archery more than anything in the world. He wanted to learn from the best teacher, Drona, who taught the princes in the royal palace.

But Drona said, "I have promised to teach only the princes."

Most children would have cried and gone home. But Ekalavya had a different idea. He went deep into the forest, made a small statue of Drona out of mud and clay, and said, "You are my teacher." Every single day, from sunrise to sunset, he practiced shooting arrows before that little clay statue. No one told him to. No one watched. He just loved learning.

Years passed. Ekalavya became so skilled that he could shoot faster and truer than anyone — even better than Prince Arjuna, Drona's most famous student.

One day, Drona came to the forest and saw what Ekalavya had done. He realized this boy had become a master all by himself. Drona asked for a teacher's gift: Ekalavya's right thumb.

Without hesitation, Ekalavya gave it. He was grateful to the teacher he had never met, because thinking of Drona had made him excellent.

**The moral:** You don't need the perfect school, the best equipment, or even a teacher in the room. If you love something enough to practice with your whole heart, you can become extraordinary. Dedication is the real magic.

*(Note: This story has a bittersweet ending — Ekalavya loses his thumb. If your child is sensitive at bedtime, you might prefer to end with "And because he was so grateful, he gave his teacher a precious gift to say thank you," without focusing on the sadness.)*
